About Me

I am currently employed as a chargeable repair officer for Bolton at Home. I have been in this position for approximately a year and six months. It is an incredibly challenging and diverse role, and I am thoroughly enjoying every moment of it as it has truly enabled me to showcase my skills and become much more confident both inside and outside of work. I have contributed immensely by implementing improvements including automating processes, improving morale, reviewing existing processes and influencing colleagues to help streamline processes. I have been involved in several projects including repair analysis, which I am currently working on as part of a team identifying flaws in the current process and developing ideas to improve the customer experience. I also worked on a new stream of work which was brought in. Initially it was only myself who was working on the implementation with a view to it being rolled out across the wider team. However I was able to identify several areas which linked closely with my own role and was able to influence my own team to take on the additional work and also management that the work should sit within the chargeable repair team. I then continued to lead this project by creating and implementing the training which is monitored and updated by myself, and as the role evolves I continue to offer improvements and lead by example. I am actively networking and advertising my skills for example I had offered to help a colleague struggling with Excel formulas which led to my involvement in the procurement process to select a DCA whereby i was asked to score and help select a new supplier. I have worked in quite a number of organisations and in hindsight maybe a little too many, sometimes working two jobs at a time, but every job has taught me new and improved existing skills. I was naive and worked to fulfil short term goals, with little thought on the future, but with the experience and skills I now possess both from employment and in my personal life I believe I am a much better employee and possess a drive and commitment to fulfil my potential. During the many years of employment I have learnt many attributes including time management and organisational skills and I have also vast experience working as part of teams learning skills such as communication and influencing. Having worked in a variety of roles, I have the ability to easily adapt to new situations and environments and have established excellent working practices. My many years experience in customer service has helped me build up my communication and conflict management skills, with both customers and work colleagues enabling me to be confident and concise whilst communicating. I am methodical and a problem solver and can quickly assess situations and make effective decisions, these skills I have gained can be applied to any role I am given. A very recent example of this is at the NHS, where I initially joined as a transaction assistant and within 6 months was promoted to supervisor, this was achieved by being pro-active and utilising my attributes and experience, but ultimately It was recognised that I am very good at what I do and when an opportunity arose I was by far the outstanding candidate. I am ambitious and have learnt that through hard work and determination any goal is achievable. My experiences and attributes, I believe, have shaped me to become the well-rounded and driven person I am today.
